📋 Code & Clearance Reference

NFPA 13 / NFSA guidance for HVLS fan installations.

⚠️
Important: Always verify final layout with local AHJ and fire protection engineer. These are general guidelines.
🔴 NFPA 13 – Critical Requirements
📏
Maximum Fan Diameter: 24 ft (NFPA 13 limit for HVLS fans under standard sprinkler design)
🎯
Sprinkler Centering: Fans shall be centered approximately between four adjacent sprinklers whenever possible
↕️
Vertical Clearance: Minimum 36 inches (3 ft) vertical clearance required from HVLS fan blade to sprinkler deflectors
🚒
Fire Alarm Interlock: All HVLS fans must be interlocked to shut down immediately upon sprinkler waterflow alarm activation
📐 General Sizing Rules of Thumb
📊
Fan Diameter vs. Ceiling Height: Maximum recommended fan diameter ≈ floor-to-blade clearance height. Example: 20 ft ceiling with 1 ft downtube = 19 ft max recommended diameter.
⬆️
Minimum Floor-to-Blade: Absolute minimum 10 ft clearance from floor to lowest blade tip
↔️
Lateral Clearances: Maintain adequate clearance from walls, columns, HVAC ducts, light fixtures, overhead doors, and other obstructions. Consult manufacturer specs for each model.
🔽
Downtubes: Longer downtubes lower blades closer to floor – always verify 36" sprinkler clearance AND 10 ft floor clearance remain met.
⚡ Electrical & Controls Notes
🔌
Vivid Air Navigator (BACnet): Requires a dedicated 120V outlet at the controller location. If not present, conduit run must be quoted.
🔧
Scope Types: Turnkey includes full power from panel. Mechanical Only = hang fan only. Mechanical + Connections = hang and wire motor, customer provides power to panel.
📡
Fire Alarm Wire: Fan Guys installers will run and terminate wire at VFD only. Customer is responsible for connection at Fire Alarm Panel (FAP).
